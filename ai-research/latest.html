AI Industry Research Notebook (long-form)

Prepared for David • Friday, 6 Mar 2026 (09:30 HKT) • A/B workflow: Builder draft + Gate review pass.

1) Executive thesis and key calls

Core thesis The AI cycle has shifted from capacity scarcity to capital efficiency. Winners over the next 12 months are less about who announces the largest spend and more about who proves durable monetization against that spend.

  1. Infra remains dominant in absolute profit dollars, but the equity multiple premium should compress unless utilization and cash conversion stay strong.
  2. Enterprise software/apps are entering the selection phase: production conversion and measured ROI will separate true platforms from “copilot feature inflation.”
  3. Power and policy are now first-order variables (not side constraints) in compute supply forecasting.
  4. High conviction: reasoning/inference demand is real. Lower conviction: pace of enterprise-wide autonomous agent adoption in regulated workflows.

3) Demand analysis (enterprise + consumer)

Facts

  • Microsoft (FY2025 Q2 call, Jan 2025) said its AI business surpassed a $13B annual revenue run rate, up 175% YoY.
  • OpenAI (NBER-linked usage study, 2025) stated ChatGPT had roughly 700M weekly active users and that about 30% of consumer usage was work-related, ~70% non-work.
  • Microsoft (FY2025 Q4 call, Jul 2025) reported Azure surpassed $75B annual revenue, up 34%.

Interpretation

Demand is not the current weak link. The weak link is proof of economic quality: renewal durability, workflow lock-in, and realized unit-cost reduction at scale.

Confidence: Medium-High on demand strength; Medium on conversion quality because disclosure quality remains uneven across vendors.

4) Supply analysis (chips, cloud, power, talent)

Facts

  • NVIDIA FY2026 Q2 (Aug 2025): revenue $46.7B (+56% YoY); data-center revenue $41.1B (+56% YoY); non-GAAP gross margin 72.7%.
  • Microsoft FY2025 Q4 call: stood up >2 gigawatts of new capacity in prior 12 months; now 400+ datacenters in 70 regions.
  • IEA Energy and AI (2025): electricity generation serving data centers projected to rise from about 460 TWh (2024) to >1,000 TWh (2030) and 1,300 TWh (2035) in its base case.

Interpretation

Chip scarcity is easing more slowly than expected, but the structural choke points now include grid interconnection, permitting, and cooling retrofit timelines. In practice, power latency is becoming a hidden capex tax.

Confidence: High on supply-demand tightness; Medium on exact regional bottleneck timing.

6) Competitive dynamics and moats by segment

  • Compute leaders: moat from integrated silicon + networking + software stack; key risk is demand concentration and policy shocks.
  • Hyperscalers: moat from global infra + enterprise distribution; key risk is capex efficiency scrutiny.
  • Enterprise software incumbents: moat from installed workflows and data gravity; key risk is shallow “assistant add-on” monetization.
  • Service integrators: moat from trust and implementation depth; key risk is labor-intensity compressing margins.

7) Financial architecture (capex, unit economics, margins, cash-flow quality)

  • Microsoft FY2025 Q2 disclosed capex incl. finance leases of $22.6B in-quarter; stated more than half cloud/AI spend was on long-lived assets.
  • NVIDIA FY2026 Q2 retained gross margin in the low-70s despite scale-up, indicating strong near-term pricing power.
  • Multiple large platforms are guiding/indicating significantly higher AI infrastructure spend into 2026 (source quality mixed; see caveats).

Judgement: the market is moving from “revenue growth only” to a two-key test: AI revenue quality and FCF durability after infra spend.

8) Geopolitics and export-control scenarios

U.S. export controls remain central to supply chain outcomes.

  • Federal Register (Jan 2025) “Framework for Artificial Intelligence Diffusion” set staged compliance timelines (initial compliance from May 15, 2025, with some delayed items to Jan 15, 2026).
  • BIS communications in 2025 signaled policy fluidity around this framework and possible replacement pathways.

9) Policy/regulatory landscape and likely pathways

  • EU AI Act rollout is progressive: prohibitions and literacy obligations applied from Feb 2025; GPAI obligations from Aug 2025; broader enforcement ramps through Aug 2026/2027.
  • Practical effect: compliance readiness is becoming part of enterprise procurement criteria, especially in regulated industries.

Likely pathway: more operational guidance and sector-specific interpretations rather than one-time “big-bang” legal clarity.

  • More bullish: broad evidence that software-layer AI revenue converts to durable high-margin recurring cash flows while infra capex growth normalizes.
