AI Industry Research Notebook (Investor Practical Edition)

Prepared for David • Tuesday, 03 Mar 2026 (09:30 HKT run) • Focus: what is proving economically durable vs what is still narrative-driven.

(1) Executive thesis and key calls

Fact Interpretation Scenario assumption

Base thesis: AI demand is still accelerating, but equity leadership is rotating from “capacity owners” to “capacity + monetization discipline owners.”

  • Fact NVIDIA reported FY2026 Q4 revenue of $68.1B (+73% y/y); FY2026 revenue $215.9B (+65% y/y) (NVIDIA, 2026-02-25).
  • Fact Microsoft FY26 Q2 revenue was $81.3B (+17%), with explicit AI-stack emphasis in management commentary (Microsoft, 2026-01-28).
  • Interpretation The market is no longer paying a premium for AI spend alone; investors now demand proof of margin retention and cash conversion.
  • Scenario If capex growth stays extreme while enterprise conversion slows, multiple compression can hit both infra and software simultaneously.

(3) Demand quality: pilots vs production

  • Fact IBM disclosed generative AI book of business >$12.5B in its 2025 results commentary (IBM, 2026-01-28).
  • Fact Oracle FY26 Q2 release highlights very large RPO growth and cloud demand signaling (Oracle, 2025-12-10).
  • Interpretation The KPI that matters now is pilot-to-production conversion in <2 quarters, not “number of pilots signed.”

(4) Supply-side reality: chips, networking, packaging, and power

  • Fact Broadcom FY2025 Q4: revenue $18.0B (+28%); guidance also remained strong (Broadcom, 2025-12-11).
  • Fact IEA projects data-center electricity demand rising materially toward 2030, with AI as a key contributor (IEA, 2025).
  • Interpretation The bottleneck stack is now system-level (power, cooling, commissioning), not just GPU die supply.
  • Scenario If utilities/interconnect timelines slip, hyperscaler monetization timing may lag despite healthy demand.

(5) Technology frontier: what is monetizing vs what is still demo theater

Fact Stanford AI Index 2025 documents rapid model-cost declines and broad capability progress (Stanford HAI, 2025).

Interpretation Falling model costs are bullish for adoption but bearish for unmoated API-only business models.

Monetizing: embedded copilots, enterprise search with permissions, inference optimization tooling.
Still fragile: broad autonomous agents in messy real-world enterprise workflows.

(7) Financial architecture: capex intensity vs cash-flow evidence

  • Fact Microsoft and Meta/Alphabet/Amazon releases continue to stress AI infrastructure investment pacing (Microsoft call, Alphabet Q4 2025 release, Meta Q4 2025 release).
  • Interpretation 2026 likely becomes a “show me AI gross-margin durability” year, not a “who spent most” year.
  • Low-confidence scenario If demand stays robust and serving-cost per task falls faster than price, current high capex can still clear acceptable returns.

(8) Geopolitics and export-control pathways

  • Fact BIS continues iterative updates/enforcement on export controls (BIS updates).
  • Interpretation Geopolitical friction should be treated as a valuation input (TAM and sell-through) rather than a tail footnote risk.
  • Scenario Managed-fragmentation is still the base case; hard decoupling remains lower probability but high-impact.

(9) Regulation and procurement: where economics are changing

  • Fact EU AI Act framework continues formalization of obligations by risk category (European Commission).
  • Interpretation Compliance cost is becoming a moat for large incumbents and high-trust enterprise vendors.
  • Scenario Procurement checklists (auditability, traceability, indemnification) increasingly gate vendor wins.

(12) Action plan and “what changes my mind” checklist

Weekly dashboard

  1. AI revenue attribution quality (management specificity)
  2. Incremental margins net of AI serving costs
  3. Pilot-to-production conversion in enterprise deployments
  4. Capacity commissioning vs demand ramp
  5. Regulatory or export-control rule changes

Would turn more bullish if

  • More companies show AI growth with stable/improving FCF conversion.
  • Demand broadens beyond top concentration points in compute supply.

Would turn more defensive if

  • Two consecutive quarters of spend acceleration without economics proof.
  • Significant policy fragmentation impairs deployment velocity.
