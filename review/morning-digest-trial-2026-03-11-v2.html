Temporary review page · Prompt rationalization trial · 2026-03-11 16:15 HKT

1) Executive framing — what changed since yesterday

The market has shifted from a pure oil-shock / stagflation scare into a relief-rally but still unresolved regime. Yesterday morning’s posture was dominated by the idea that Middle East disruption would keep oil elevated, complicate inflation, delay easier Fed conditions, and pressure long-duration equities. Overnight, that pressure partially eased as crude fell sharply on de-escalation rhetoric. That matters because your watchlist is much more exposed to discount-rate sensitivity and risk-premium compression than to commodity upside.

But the key organizational point is this: the overnight improvement did not come from a cleaner growth backdrop, better economic data, or an obviously easier Fed path. It came mainly from a reduction in immediate energy-stress pricing. So the market got relief without getting clarity. That distinction matters because semis, mega-cap tech, and AI-beta can rally sharply in that setup, but they also remain vulnerable if oil or rates turn back up.

The one meaningful single-name development that stands apart from the macro tape is Tesla. Reuters-level reporting suggests the U.S. Autopilot probe may be taking a more serious securities / wire-fraud angle. If that is confirmed more fully, it becomes a valuation and legal-regulatory issue, not just another FSD headline.

2) Macro / policy / regime map

Geopolitics: the market is still trading the Middle East first. The important update is that the market moved from pricing a live supply-shock scenario toward pricing the possibility that the conflict ends faster than feared. That reduced the immediate oil-risk premium, but it is still fragile because the underlying geopolitical resolution is not yet hard or verified.

Oil: oil remains the most important macro transmission channel for your watchlist right now. Higher oil would revive inflation anxiety and push the market back toward higher-for-longer rates and tighter financial conditions. Lower oil does the opposite: it eases the pressure on growth multiples and supports semis / big tech / broad risk assets.

Fed / rates: the Fed backdrop is still awkward. Even if growth softens, an energy shock can stop the market from getting a clean “bad data = lower yields = buy tech” reaction. That means the watchlist is still heavily exposed to the bond market’s interpretation of inflation persistence. The overnight move helped because energy stress eased, not because the policy outlook suddenly became easy.

Economy data: there is no major fresh economic release in this trial note changing the regime by itself. The point is that growth/inflation interpretation is still being driven more by oil and war headlines than by a clean new macro-data surprise. That keeps the market headline-sensitive and unstable even during rebounds.

Bottom macro read: this is now best described as a relief-with-fragility regime. Better than yesterday morning, but not structurally safe.

3) Cross-asset linkages — what moves what

  • Geopolitics → oil: the faster the market believes conflict risk is easing, the faster crude gives back panic premium.
  • Oil → inflation expectations: lower oil reduces the immediate fear that inflation re-accelerates just as growth looks shakier.
  • Inflation expectations → yields / Fed path: if energy pressure eases, the market has more room to price a friendlier rates backdrop. If energy pressure returns, that window narrows fast.
  • Yields / discount rates → semis and mega-cap tech: this is the main transmission channel for AVGO, NVDA, TSM, MSFT, GOOG, AAPL, and to a lesser extent TSLA.
  • Risk appetite → smaller-cap thematic names: PLTR, RKLB, RDW, KTOS, BBAI, ONDS can benefit from a friendlier tape, but they still need stock-specific proof because they are less protected by sheer balance-sheet strength and market dominance.
  • Single-name legal/regulatory shocks → valuation reset: TSLA is the clearest example this morning. If autonomy/legal risk becomes more severe, the name can underperform even in a broader relief tape.

The point of this section is not just “what is linked to what,” but which link matters most today: right now, oil → inflation/rates → semis/mega-cap tech is still the dominant chain.

  1. Base case (45%) — relief holds, but selectively. Oil stays off the panic highs, yields remain calmer, and the market continues to reward liquid quality over lower-quality beta. Effect: best for AVGO/NVDA/TSM/MSFT/GOOG/AAPL; only cautious help for smaller speculative names.
