Practical glossary (quick read)

  • Real yields: interest rates adjusted for inflation; when they rise fast, expensive growth stocks usually face pressure.
  • Credit spread: extra yield investors demand for corporate debt vs government debt; widening spread often means rising risk stress.
  • Breadth: how many stocks are participating in an up move; weak breadth means index strength may be fragile.
  • Invalidation: predefined point where your thesis is considered wrong and you reduce/exit risk.
