Oil

Oil remains the cleanest transmission variable. If oil were merely spiking intraday and then fading, the market could look through it. It is not doing that yet.

Fed / rates

The next FOMC meeting still caps aggressive risk-taking. Reuters' Friday market wrap noted traders were pricing only about 20 bps of Fed easing this year versus roughly 50 bps a month earlier. That is the real policy message: the market is not debating imminent hikes, but it is re-pricing away the easy-cut cushion that would normally support long-duration growth. High oil plus sticky core inflation keeps the Fed window hawkish by default.

Economy-data context

Friday's data mix did not solve the policy dilemma. Headline PCE at 2.8% YoY was a touch cooler than expected, but core PCE at 3.1% is still too high for comfort and Q4 GDP revised down to 0.7% reminds the market that growth is not strong enough to shrug off tighter financial conditions forever. That is classic uncomfortable late-cycle texture: not a clean inflation victory, not a clean growth engine.

Bottom macro read

Stagflation-lite pressure is still the right near-term frame. Not full panic, not a crash call, but enough oil-and-rates pressure to keep the burden of proof on every upside thesis. That means the right lens is selective triage, not blanket bullishness.
