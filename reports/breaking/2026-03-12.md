### Oil is re-accelerating even after the IEA’s record reserve release

Reuters reported that oil rebounded as markets doubted whether the IEA’s planned 400 million-barrel strategic-reserve release could offset Iran-war supply risk, and separately that Iran’s military said the world should be prepared for $200 oil. The second point is rhetoric, not a price print, but together they mark a meaningful shift: the market is not treating policy action as enough to fully cap the shock.

**Why it matters**
This raises the odds that the watchlist stays trapped under an oil → inflation → yields/risk-premium squeeze instead of getting a clean relief rally. The key new information is not just war noise; it is that even the biggest reserve-release response on record is not yet restoring confidence.

**Watchlist impact**
Direct: **NVDA, AVGO, TSM, GOOG, AAPL, TSLA, PLTR, COHR, LITE** face renewed macro pressure if higher crude keeps long-duration multiples under stress.
Second-order: **KTOS, RDW, RKLB, ONDS** can hold up relatively better if conflict persistence keeps defense/autonomy demand and positioning firm, but that is a relative effect, not a guaranteed upside call.
