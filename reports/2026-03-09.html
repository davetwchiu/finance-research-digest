Regime diagnosis

The best way to describe the regime is defensive dispersion with inflation friction. Growth anxiety alone would support a cleaner “buy duration / buy quality” stance. But this is not a pure growth scare. There is still oil and geopolitical pressure in the system, and policy transmission through tariffs and bond-market risk premia remains unstable. That weakens the usual playbook where falling growth expectations immediately rescue expensive equities.

For the watchlist, this keeps a split market alive. Cash-rich, mega-cap compounders can remain operationally strong while still suffering entry-quality problems if the market decides the discount rate is too high. Semiconductor names get an extra layer of complexity from tariff and Taiwan policy language. Defence and space may hold up better on thematic flows, but those are still vulnerable if macro tightening spills into broad risk reduction.

The internal deterministic layer still matters here. Even if headline interpretation is noisy, the signal pipeline provides an anchor for breadth, momentum, and realized-volatility state. Cross-asset linkage map

Jobs → Fed path → front-end rates: weaker labor data raises cut expectations, but only if inflation risk does not immediately offset it.

Oil / geopolitics → inflation expectations → term premium: if crude holds up because of geopolitical risk rather than demand strength, the market still has to price inflation persistence and event risk.

Tariffs / trade policy → Treasuries and semis: tariff shocks do not just affect trade-sensitive sectors; they can alter bond-market risk premia and therefore the valuation framework for the whole growth complex.

Taiwan / semiconductor policy → AI hardware stack: TSM, NVDA, AVGO, LITE, and related names remain exposed to language shifts around tariffs, cross-border industrial policy, and tool or wafer supply conditions.

Credit / liquidity → speculative beta: when macro ambiguity rises, the market tends to punish lower-liquidity stories first, even if the long-term narrative survives.
