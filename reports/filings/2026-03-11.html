ONDS — 8-K Primary filing read Exhibit 99.2 press release read

Filed: 2026-03-11 · Company: Ondas Inc.

What happened
Ondas filed an 8-K tied to its acquisition of BIRD Aerosystems. The filing says Ondas also filed a prospectus supplement covering the possible resale of 6,933,110 common shares that were issued to certain stockholders in connection with the deal.

Layman version
Ondas just bought a defense-tech company that already has real military and security deployments. That is the strategic upside. But because part of the deal involved issuing stock, the market also now has to think about dilution and possible selling pressure from those new holders.

Why it matters now
The press release is more meaningful than the generic 8-K label. It says BIRD brings airborne missile-protection systems and ISR technology already installed on 700+ aircraft across 40+ aircraft types, including users tied to the U.S. Army, NATO, APAC air forces, and UN aviation fleets. That is a real capability step-up, not just a concept pitch.

What changed vs before
Before this, Ondas was more centered on autonomous aerial systems, counter-UAS, robotics, and private wireless. After this deal, it is explicitly pushing into airborne missile protection and airborne ISR/mission systems. The business mix becomes more defense-heavy and more mission-system oriented.

Positives

  • Adds a proven installed base instead of early-stage promise only.
  • Expands Ondas from drones/counter-UAS into aircraft protection and broader airborne mission systems.
  • The press release repeatedly frames this as a way to build a more complete multi-domain defense stack.
  • Management said it will provide more BIRD outlook details on the March 25 earnings call, so there is a near-term follow-up catalyst.

Risks / red flags

  • The filing explicitly covers resale registration for 6.93M shares issued in the acquisition. Even if holders do not sell immediately, the overhang is real.
  • We verified the acquisition announcement and resale-registration language, but this filing did not give a full investor-grade financial breakdown of BIRD inside the main body we read.
  • Integration risk is obvious: folding an Israel-based defense supplier into Ondas’ platform and proving cross-sell/synergy are not automatic.

What to watch next

  • Any disclosed purchase price / financial contribution details on the March 25 call.
  • Whether Ondas quantifies revenue, backlog, or margin contribution from BIRD.
  • Whether the new registered shares begin to create stock-pressure concerns.

KTOS — 8-K Primary filing read

Filed: 2026-03-06 · Company: Kratos Defense & Security Solutions, Inc.

What happened
Kratos disclosed that it completed the Orbit Technologies acquisition on March 2. Orbit is now an indirect wholly owned subsidiary of Kratos.

Layman version
This is the deal-closing filing. The market no longer has to guess whether the acquisition will happen. It happened, and Kratos paid about $352.7 million in cash from its own balance sheet.

Why it matters now
For defense names, completed capability expansion matters more than announced intent. A signed deal can still break; a closed deal changes the actual business immediately.

What changed vs before
Back in November, investors only had the merger agreement. This filing confirms the close, confirms the cash outlay, and confirms that Orbit option holders were cashed out under the merger terms. In plain English: the transaction moved from plan to reality.

Positives

  • Definitive close removes execution uncertainty around this acquisition.
  • Paid with cash on hand rather than visibly scrambling for new financing in this filing.
  • Adds a real operating business, not just a memorandum of understanding.

Risks / red flags

  • $352.7M cash is meaningful, so investors should watch balance-sheet flexibility after the close.
  • This 8-K confirms closing mechanics, but it does not by itself give a full plain-English synergy map or detailed near-term financial impact.
  • As with any defense acquisition, integration and contract-retention risk still matter after close.

What to watch next

  • Next earnings call for revenue, backlog, and margin contribution from Orbit.
  • Whether management frames Orbit as mainly growth-accretive, capability-accretive, or margin-accretive.
  • Any signs the acquisition changes Kratos’ capital allocation or debt posture.

AVGO — 8-K Primary filing read Exhibit 99.1 earnings release read

Filed: 2026-03-04 · Company: Broadcom Inc.

What happened
Broadcom used its 8-K to furnish Q1 FY2026 earnings and guidance. The real substance sat in Exhibit 99.1, and it was strong.

Layman version
Broadcom basically said: the AI engine is still ripping, overall revenue is hitting records, cash generation remains huge, and next quarter guidance is even stronger.

Why it matters now
This is a hard-data filing, not a story stock press release. The exhibit reported Q1 revenue of $19.311B (+29% YoY), GAAP net income of $7.349B, adjusted EBITDA of $13.128B (68% margin), and free cash flow of $8.010B. Management also said AI revenue was $8.4B (+106% YoY) and guided Q2 revenue to about $22.0B.

What changed vs before
The notable change is the acceleration in the AI side of the business. Semiconductor solutions rose 52% year over year to $12.515B, while infrastructure software grew only 1% to $6.796B. The mix is leaning harder into semis/AI rather than software carrying the story.

Positives

  • Big revenue beat-style profile: record quarter plus stronger next-quarter guide.
  • AI demand was not just good; management said it was above forecast.
  • Cash generation remained enormous, which supports dividends and buybacks.
  • The company also announced a new $10B share repurchase program.

Risks / red flags

  • The business is becoming even more dependent on AI semiconductor demand staying hot.
  • Infrastructure software growth was basically flat, so the non-AI side is not carrying momentum.
  • When expectations get this high, any later guide wobble can hit hard even if results remain objectively good.

What to watch next

  • Whether Q2 actually lands near the $22B guide.
  • Whether AI revenue keeps compounding without customer concentration worries becoming louder.
  • Whether software growth reaccelerates or the story becomes too one-legged.

NVDA — 8-K

The filing was about the adoption of NVIDIA’s fiscal 2027 variable compensation plan for executives. It set performance goals around fiscal 2027 revenue and disclosed target cash-award opportunities, including $4.0M for CEO Jensen Huang at base-plan achievement and $1.5M for several other named executives. Useful governance disclosure, but not a direct operating update.

Why it matters: it shows the board is still tying senior cash comp to revenue execution, which fits the company’s growth posture. Why it matters less right now: it does not tell us anything new about demand, margins, customers, or product execution.

GOOG — 8-K

Alphabet’s filing approved a new triennial CEO equity package for Sundar Pichai. His salary stays at $2M with no annual bonus, but the new package includes performance stock units, regular stock units, and private-company bet-linked units tied to Waymo and Wing. On-target values disclosed in the filing were roughly $126M in Alphabet PSUs, $84M in Alphabet GSUs, plus about $130M for Waymo BPUs and $45M for Wing BPUs.

Why it matters: this shows Alphabet is explicitly trying to tie CEO incentives to both core stock performance and value creation in big internal bets. Why it matters less right now: it is still mainly a compensation filing, not a business update.
