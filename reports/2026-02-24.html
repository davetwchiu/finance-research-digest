Macro Regime Deep Context

Global macro is in a late-cycle disinflation tug-of-war, not a clean risk-on trend. US 10Y is 4.03% (-0.67% 1w), with equities diverging (S&P 500 +0.02% 1w, Nasdaq +0.36% 1w). VIX at 21.01 (+1.99% 1w) says volatility is contained but not complacent. Oil at 66.29 (+5.41% 1w) and DXY 97.72 (+0.87% 1w) keep the inflation/financial-conditions channel alive.

Cross-asset read-through: lower real-rate pressure supports quality growth duration, but the floor in oil and stable dollar prevent a full multiple-expansion regime. This is a conditional risk-on setup, not an indiscriminate one.

  • Rates channel: if 10Y breaks higher while VIX lifts, high-beta names likely underperform first.
  • Commodity channel: sustained oil rebound can re-open inflation-risk hedging and flatten equity breadth.
  • FX channel: firmer DXY historically tightens global liquidity and can weigh on cyclicals/ex-US demand expectations.

Policy Path Analysis

Policy path remains a three-body problem: Fed reaction function (growth vs inflation), US fiscal/defense outlays, and export-control enforcement for AI/advanced compute. Base case is slower easing and data-dependent communication; upside inflation surprises delay cuts and pressure duration-sensitive growth. Any tightening in chip/export rules would hit semiconductor supply chains first, then AI capex expectations.

Scenario tree (1-3 months):

  • Scenario A – Soft-landing disinflation (45%): gradual easing bias, stable yields, constructive for AI infrastructure leaders and cash-rich quality franchises.
  • Scenario B – Sticky inflation/no-cut delay (35%): front-end reprices higher, valuation headwind for long-duration growth, favor selective value/insurers/cash-flow quality.
  • Scenario C – Growth downshift + policy support (20%): yields compress, defensives and mega-cap quality outperform, speculative beta rallies become shorter and more tactical.

Policy checkpoint list: next CPI/PCE prints, labor slack trend, Treasury issuance mix, export-control updates, and US defense budget cadence.

Portfolio Construction Implications

  • Position sizing discipline: high-signal small/mid-caps should run with tighter invalidations and staggered profit-taking at T1/T2.
  • Correlation warning: when rates and dollar rise together, many "idiosyncratic" growth names trade as one factor.
  • Execution: prioritize close-confirmed breakouts; fade failed breakouts quickly.
