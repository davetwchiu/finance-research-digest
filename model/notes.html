Atlas model notes

What this site is actually doing, what to trust most, and where the weak spots still are.

Last updated: 2026-03-10 14:40 HKT

What Atlas is for

Atlas is not trying to be a full trading terminal. Its job is narrower and more useful: turn a defined watchlist plus macro/policy context into a decision-ready daily briefing with explicit freshness signals, plain-language interpretation, and clear uncertainty labels.

Best useMacro framingWatchlist triageProof-first research

Model stack

  1. Local signal pipeline — deterministic signal generation from the watchlist into signals_local.json.
  2. Atlas snapshot — condensed local-intelligence layer for homepage breadth, risk, and movers.
  3. Deep analysis — regime and triage summary that explains what the local signal layer is implying.
  4. Ticker page generator — per-name pages combining technical setup, business proof, catalyst calendar, and risk map.
  5. Daily macro digest — long-form regime note with scenario tree, action matrix, and risk checklist.

What to trust most

  • Highest confidence: freshness markers, local signal state, required-section checks, and whether a page is explicitly flagged provisional.
  • Medium confidence: macro/policy framing when linked to primary or top-tier sources.
  • Lower confidence: issuer-specific conclusions when fundamentals refresh or catalyst verification is degraded.

Rule of thumb: if Atlas marks a page PROVISIONAL, take that literally. It means the structure may be complete while the proof density is still not good enough for full conviction.

Current limitations

  • Yahoo-based fundamentals refresh can fail with 401 errors, which weakens some company-level business evidence.
  • Some investor-relations pages and transcripts block automated fetches, so certain claims must stay conservative unless directly verified.
  • Breaking-news coverage is optimized for materiality, not completeness. It is meant to filter noise, not archive every mention.
  • The site is strongest when used as a research triage layer, not as a standalone execution engine.

How to read the watchlist pages

  1. Start with the Layman summary.
  2. Check the quality gate state and reasons.
  3. Use last verified time, freshness state, and evidence quality score before trusting the conclusion.
  4. Treat the actionable setup as a scenario map, not an instruction to trade.
